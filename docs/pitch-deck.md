## Slide 2 — The Problem

# Great stories, told to almost no one

Muzium Negara holds the national story — 200,000 years of it, across four permanent galleries and hundreds of artifacts. But the experience of *understanding* it hasn't kept pace with the visitor.

- **Static labels, limited depth.** A one-line caption can't answer "why does this matter?" Visitors walk past *Manusia Perak*, the *Avalokiteshvara*, the *Pen Kemerdekaan* without the story behind them.
- **Guides don't scale.** Human docents are wonderful — and scarce. They can't be beside every visitor, in every language, on a busy weekend.
- **Language barriers.** International tourists and younger Malaysians often want context in their own words. Bilingual, on-demand explanation is rare.
- **Young visitors disengage.** School groups and families need a warm, story-first voice, not a wall of text.

**The result:** a world-class collection that too many visitors experience as objects behind glass rather than a story they can follow.

*Speaker note: Anchor with a concrete moment — a family standing in front of the Perak Man replica with no idea it's the oldest, most complete skeleton in Southeast Asia.*

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution

# Kancil: a museum guide in everyone's pocket

Kancil is an **AI chat companion** that answers visitor questions about Muzium Negara's galleries and artifacts — accurately, warmly, and in the visitor's language.

- **Ask anything.** "What is the Bunga Emas?" "Which gallery covers Merdeka?" Kancil answers conversationally, then points the way.
- **Grounded, never bluffing.** Every answer is drawn from a curated knowledge base sourced from the official museum content. When Kancil isn't sure, she says so — no invented dates or artifacts.
- **Two languages, one tap.** Full **English and Bahasa Melayu**, with a corner toggle that switches the language Kancil replies in.
- **A character kids remember.** *Sang Kancil* — in her *tengkolok* and *batik* sampin — turns facts into stories. Warm, playful, trustworthy.
- **Zero friction.** It's a web app. No download, no queue. Scan a QR code and start.

*Speaker note: Emphasise "grounded" — the single biggest fear with AI in a national institution is hallucination. Kancil is explicitly built to answer only from vetted museum knowledge.*

## Slide 5 — How To Use

# From gallery wall to conversation in one scan

**For the visitor — three steps:**
1. **Scan** a QR code on the gallery wall, ticket, or brochure (or visit the web link).
2. **Ask** a question — by voice-friendly text or by tapping a suggested prompt.
3. **Explore** — Kancil answers and guides them to the right gallery and artifact.

**For the museum — nothing to install:**
- Runs in any phone browser. No app store, no downloads, no visitor accounts.
- Deployed on modern cloud hosting; updates go live instantly.
- Museum staff update the knowledge base as exhibits change — Kancil learns the new content immediately.

**Deployment surfaces:**
- QR codes beside key artifacts and at gallery entrances
- The museum website and ticketing confirmation
- Optional in-gallery tablets / kiosks for visitors without phones

*Speaker note: The "nothing to install" point matters for procurement — no IT burden on the museum, no per-device licensing.*
